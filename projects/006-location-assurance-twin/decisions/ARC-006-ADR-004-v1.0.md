**Carry telemetry as semantic YANG-Push into a message broker (Kafka/Redpanda), preserving YANG semantics and making records addressable by subscription/xpath/element via the IETF NMOP message-key scheme, so consumers pull only scoped slices.**

## 3. Context and Problem Statement

### 3.1 Problem Description

The closed loop is only as good as its input signal. The feed must (a) preserve the **YANG semantics** of telemetry from device to graph, (b) carry a **causal (data-plane)** signal, not just black-box aggregates, and (c) let many consumers pull only the **scoped slice** they need at scale. A naive metrics feed or a direct device→consumer link cannot do all three.

**Problem statement as a question**: How should network telemetry reach the correlation/assessment layers while preserving semantics, carrying causal signal, and scaling by scoped consumption?

### 3.2 Why This Decision Is Needed

- **Business context**: BR-002 (causal observability depends on the feed).
- **Technical context**: FR-001 (L0 feed), INT-001 (device YANG-Push → broker), NFR-P-002 (causal signal), NFR-S-001 (partitioning/scoped consume), NFR-A-002 (subscription health).
- **Regulatory context**: none directly; feed auth (OAuth where control-plane reached) per NFR-SEC-004.

## 4. Decision Drivers (Forces)

### 4.1 Technical Drivers

- **Semantics preserved end-to-end** (HLD P6): telemetry must carry its YANG meaning device→graph. FR-001, NFR-P-002.
- **Scoped consumption at scale**: broker partitioning via message-key hash+modulo; consumers pull xpath/element slices. NFR-S-001.
- **Decoupling + replay**: a broker decouples producers from consumers and enables fan-out/replay. PRIN 11 (Async).
- **Subscription health as a safety precondition**: a dead feed must not read as conformance. NFR-A-002.

### 4.2 Business Drivers

- **Reproducible, standards-aligned feed** (SD-8/G-6): NMOP alignment makes the pattern collaboratable and citable.

### 4.3 Regulatory & Compliance Drivers

- **Feed/transport auth** (NFR-SEC-004): OAuth where a control-plane is reached; no raw config authored.
- **Open standards**: IETF RFC 8639/8640/8641; NMOP message-key draft.

## 5. Considered Options

### Option 1: Semantic YANG-Push → broker, message-key addressable — CHOSEN

**Description**: L0 subscribes to device YANG-Push (RFC 8639/8641, NETCONF transport RFC 8640); writes typed records to Kafka/Redpanda topics named per the NMOP message-key scheme; consumers pull xpath/element-scoped slices. Subscription health is a precondition for ACTING.
**Implementation approach**: feed component with a kafka/redpanda producer; topics keyed per the draft; rig may stub devices via Netopeer2/Sysrepo; NMOP draft revisions pinned in README; scheme isolated behind the feed adapter.
**Wardley Evolution Stage**: feed pattern Custom-Built over Commodity broker (Kafka/Redpanda) + Product (YANG-Push).

#### Good (Pros)

- ✅ Preserves YANG semantics end-to-end (FR-001, NFR-P-002).
- ✅ Scales by partitioning + scoped consumption (NFR-S-001).
- ✅ Decoupling, fan-out, replay (PRIN 11); multiple consumers (L1 + others).
- ✅ Standards-aligned (RFC 8639/8641 + NMOP), collaboratable (SD-8).

#### Bad (Cons)

- ❌ Depends on a work-in-progress NMOP draft (R-6).
- ❌ Broker is an extra component to run.
- ❌ Subscription-health handling needed to avoid silent-feed false "all clear" (R-8).

#### Cost Analysis

CAPEX ~£0 (Kafka/Redpanda OSS). OPEX: broker + feed component on the rig. TCO: feed maintenance + draft tracking.

#### GDS Service Standard Impact

| Point | Impact | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 4. Open standards | Positive | RFC 8639/8641; NMOP draft |
| 9. Technology | Positive | Scalable async telemetry |

### Option 2: Direct YANG-Push device → consumer (no broker)

**Description**: Consumer subscribes directly to each device's YANG-Push stream.
**Wardley**: Product.
**Good**: ✅ Fewer components; lower latency for one consumer.
**Bad**: ❌ No fan-out/partitioning/replay; ❌ tight producer↔consumer coupling (PRIN 10/11 violated); ❌ does not scale to many consumers/devices (NFR-S-001 unmet).
**Cost**: low build, poor scale/decoupling.

### Option 3: Poll-based / SNMP / black-box metrics feed

**Description**: Periodic polling or SNMP/aggregate metrics instead of semantic YANG-Push.
**Wardley**: Commodity.
**Good**: ✅ Ubiquitous, simple.
**Bad**: ❌ Loses YANG semantics; ❌ black-box/aggregate, not causal (NFR-P-002 unmet — symptom→cause needs causal signal); ❌ not addressable by xpath/element.
**Cost**: low build, fails the causal-observability requirement.

### Option 4: Do Nothing (Baseline)

**Description**: No purpose-built feed; ad-hoc ingestion.
**Good**: ✅ No upfront work.
**Bad**: ❌ No semantic, scalable, causal feed → the closed loop has no trustworthy input; BR-002 unmet.

## 6. Decision Outcome

### 6.1 Chosen Option

**"Option 1: Semantic YANG-Push → broker, message-key addressable."**

### 6.2 Y-Statement

> **In the context of** a closed loop whose value depends on semantic, causal, scalable telemetry,
> **facing** options that either lose YANG semantics, fail to scale, or tightly couple producers and consumers,
> **we decided for** semantic YANG-Push into a message broker, addressable by the NMOP message-key scheme with scoped consumption,
> **to achieve** end-to-end semantics, causal signal, and partitioned scale with decoupling and replay,
> **accepting** a dependency on a work-in-progress NMOP draft (pinned + encapsulated) and the need to treat subscription health as a precondition for acting.

### 6.3 Justification

1. **Only Option 1 satisfies all three feed requirements** — semantics (Option 3 fails), scale/decoupling (Option 2 fails), and causal signal (Option 3 fails).
2. **Standards-aligned and collaboratable** (RFC 8639/8641 + NMOP; SD-8/G-6).
3. **Draft risk is bounded** by pinning revisions and isolating the scheme behind the feed adapter (R-6).

**Stakeholder consensus**: L0 Feed Owner + Lead Architect; Assurance Architect confirms the monitor phase consumes from it. **Risk appetite**: technology appetite Medium; the draft dependency (R-6) is within it with controls.
