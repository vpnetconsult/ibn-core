## 3. Context and Problem Statement

### 3.1 Problem Description

The location foundation underpins every correlation/dispatch/intent query. It must be **authoritative** (a recognised standard, not invented) and **provenance-traceable**, or the design will not survive an architecture panel or publication. A prior DTDL modelling carried at least three joint-name errors.

**Problem statement as a question**: Which location model is authoritative, and how do we keep modelling-convenience constructs distinguishable from standard ones?

### 3.2 Why This Decision Is Needed

- **Business context**: BR-005 (standards & provenance discipline).

## 5. Considered Options

### Option 1: GB922 v24.0 + provenance tagging — CHOSEN

**Description**: Adopt SID GB922 v24.0 Place tree + the six corrected v24 joints; tag elements `[SID v24]`/`[MODEL]`; promote `[MODEL]` civic/indoor edges to the named v24 `GeographicLocationRelationship` / `LocalLocationRelationship` before publication.
**Implementation approach**: Minimal OWL (~15 classes in use) stamped GB922 v24.0; provenance predicate on every element; deviation report records approximations.
**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Product/Commodity (mature industry standard).

#### Good (Pros)

- ✅ Authoritative + current (corrects 3 DTDL errors) — defensible (BR-005).
- ✅ Native civic hierarchy + cross-domain joints (DR-001/002).
- ✅ Provenance traceable to RDF (NFR-C-003, DR-005).

#### Bad (Cons)

- ❌ Minimal-OWL subset risks being mistaken for full ABE (R-3).
- ❌ `[MODEL]` civic/indoor edges need promotion before publication (R-2).

#### Cost Analysis

CAPEX ~£0 (open standard). OPEX: ontology maintenance + deviation report. TCO: standards-tracking effort.

#### GDS Service Standard Impact

| Point | Impact | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 4. Open standards | Positive | SID GB922 is an open industry standard |

### Option 2: Bespoke / DTDL location model

**Description**: Keep the prior DTDL-style ad-hoc model.
**Wardley**: Custom.
**Good**: ✅ Already drafted.
**Bad**: ❌ Carried ≥ 3 joint-name errors; ❌ non-authoritative — fails review (BR-005); ❌ no standard provenance.
**Cost**: low build, high credibility cost.

### Option 3: Generic geospatial ontology only (e.g., GeoSPARQL)

**Description**: Model geometry/geography with GeoSPARQL/OGC, without SID.
**Wardley**: Product.
**Good**: ✅ Strong geometry support.
**Bad**: ❌ Lacks SID cross-domain joints (Service/Resource/Product/Stock→Place) — the correlation backbone; ❌ not telco service-model aligned.
**Cost**: medium; loses the joints that make correlation work.

### Option 4: Do Nothing (Baseline)

**Description**: Invent the civic/location hierarchy ad hoc.
**Good**: ✅ No standards study.
**Bad**: ❌ Re-inventing a native standard (HLD warns against it); ❌ non-authoritative; ❌ R-2 realised.

## 6. Decision Outcome

### 6.1 Chosen Option

**"Option 1: GB922 v24.0 + provenance tagging."**

### 6.2 Y-Statement

> **In the context of** a location foundation that must be authoritative and defensible at panel/publication,
> **facing** a prior bespoke model with corrected errors and the risk of non-authoritative invention,
> **we decided for** TM Forum SID GB922 v24.0 (Place tree + six v24 joints) with `[SID v24]`/`[MODEL]` provenance carried to RDF,
> **to achieve** standards fidelity, native cross-domain joints, and traceable provenance,
> **accepting** that the minimal OWL subset and `[MODEL]` civic/indoor edges must be scoped-stated and promoted before publication.

### 6.3 Justification

1. **Only Option 1 is authoritative *and* carries the cross-domain joints** correlation needs — Option 2 fails credibility, Option 3 lacks the joints.
2. **Provenance discipline** makes the design auditable (SD-2/G-5, NFR-C-003).
3. **DTDL error correction** (three joint names) already done in HLD §4.3.

**Stakeholder consensus**: Standards Reviewer accountable; rig-pragmatism reconciled by the promote-before-publication plan (REQ Conflict C-4). **Risk appetite**: standards-credibility appetite Very Low; this option meets it once `[MODEL]` edges are promoted.
