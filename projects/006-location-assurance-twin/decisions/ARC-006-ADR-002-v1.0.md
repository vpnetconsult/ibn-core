## 1. Decision Title

**Master only static service-location; never persist live per-subscriber serving location — resolve it on demand, TTL-projected by a short-lived job, with the boundary enforced in both schema and code.**

## 3. Context and Problem Statement

### 3.1 Problem Description

Correlation (Q-CORRELATE) and dispatch (Q-DISPATCH) sometimes need *where a subscriber is being served right now*. The convenient implementation — store per-subscriber serving location in the operational graph — would create durable location PII, the single highest-impact privacy exposure in the system.

**Problem statement as a question**: How do we satisfy live-location needs without mastering durable per-subscriber location PII?

### 3.2 Why This Decision Is Needed

- **Business context**: BR-006 (privacy-by-default posture).
- **Technical context**: FR-014 (on-demand transient resolution), NFR-C-001 (data minimisation), DR-004 (transient boundary).
- **Regulatory context**: GDPR Art 5(1)(c) (minimisation) and Art 25 (data protection by design); Malaysia PDPA 2010.

### 3.3 Supporting Links

- **Requirements**: BR-006, FR-014, NFR-C-001, DR-004; Conflict C-2.
- **Stakeholder**: SD-6 (DPO) → Goal G-4 → Outcome O-3.
- **Risk**: R-7 (durable per-subscriber location leakage) — highest inherent risk (15).
- **Source HLD**: §4.4 (transient boundary), §10 D7.
- **Related ADRs**: ADR-001 (the stores this boundary constrains).

## 4. Decision Drivers (Forces)

### 4.1 Technical Drivers

- **Schema-enforceable minimisation**: the boundary must be structural, not policy — the LPG schema must make durable subscriber location impossible to persist. NFR-C-001, DR-004.
- **Live-location still needed at point of use**: dispatch/correlation need current location transiently. FR-014.

### 4.2 Business Drivers

- **Regulatory acceptability** (BR-006): no durable location PII is a precondition for operator/PDPA acceptance and trust (SD-6/G-4).

### 4.3 Regulatory & Compliance Drivers

- **GDPR Art 5(1)(c) / Art 25**; **Malaysia PDPA 2010** (data minimisation, purpose limitation).
- **GDS Service Standard** (good-practice lens): Point 5 (security/privacy by design).

### 4.4 Alignment to Architecture Principles

| Principle | Alignment | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| PRIN 6 — Data Sovereignty & Governance | ✅ Supports | Minimisation + transient handling of subscriber location |
| PRIN 4 — Security by Design | ✅ Supports | Boundary enforced in schema + code, not bolted on |
| PRIN 8 — Single Source of Truth | ✅ Supports | Static service-location is master; transient projection is explicitly non-master |

## 5. Considered Options

### Option 1: On-demand TTL projection only (no durable subscriber location) — CHOSEN

**Description**: Durable master = static service-location (which site anchors a service, which place hosts a resource). Live per-subscriber location is resolved on demand and projected with a TTL by a short-lived job, never written as master data.
**Implementation approach**: LPG schema has no durable subscriber-location node/edge type; a transient projection store/job carries TTL; store-inspection test asserts none persists.
**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Custom-Built (privacy pattern) over Commodity stores.

#### Good (Pros)

- ✅ Satisfies live-location needs (FR-014) **and** minimisation (NFR-C-001).
- ✅ Structural enforcement (schema) — not reliant on discipline.
- ✅ Lowest privacy/regulatory exposure (R-7 → residual Low).

#### Bad (Cons)

- ❌ On-demand resolution adds latency/complexity vs a durable lookup.
- ❌ TTL job is extra moving part to test.

#### Cost Analysis

CAPEX ~£0 (open-source). OPEX: a short-lived projection job. TCO: dominated by the store-inspection test + TTL job maintenance.

#### GDS Service Standard Impact

| Point | Impact | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 5. Security/Privacy | Positive | Privacy by design |

### Option 2: Durable per-subscriber location store

**Description**: Persist current serving location per subscriber for fast lookup.
**Wardley**: Product.
**Good**: ✅ Simplest/fastest correlation & dispatch.
**Bad**: ❌ Durable location PII — breaches minimisation (NFR-C-001) and BR-006; ❌ high regulatory/trust exposure (R-7 stays High); ❌ contradicts PRIN 6.
**Cost**: low engineering, high compliance/liability cost.

### Option 3: No subscriber location at all (static service-location only)

**Description**: Never resolve live subscriber location, even transiently.
**Wardley**: Custom.
**Good**: ✅ Zero subscriber-location handling.
**Bad**: ❌ Breaks dispatch/correlation use cases that genuinely need current location (FR-011/FR-007 weakened); ❌ over-restrictive.
**Cost**: lowest, but loses required capability.

### Option 4: Do Nothing (Baseline)

**Description**: No explicit boundary; let implementers decide ad hoc.
**Good**: ✅ No upfront design.
**Bad**: ❌ Durable subscriber location creeps in by convenience (R-7 realised); ❌ no minimisation guarantee; ❌ fails BR-006.

## 6. Decision Outcome

### 6.1 Chosen Option

**"Option 1: On-demand TTL projection only."**

### 6.2 Y-Statement

> **In the context of** an assurance system that needs live subscriber location only at point of use,
> **facing** the privacy/regulatory hazard of durable location PII,
> **we decided for** mastering only static service-location and resolving live per-subscriber location on demand with a TTL, enforced in schema and code,
> **to achieve** correlation/dispatch capability with PDPA/GDPR data-minimisation,
> **accepting** added on-demand-resolution latency and a TTL job to maintain and test.

### 6.3 Justification

1. **It is the only option that satisfies both** the operational need (FR-014) and minimisation (NFR-C-001) — Option 2 fails privacy, Option 3 fails capability.
2. **Structural enforcement** (schema) makes the boundary robust against future shortcuts (R-7 control).
3. **DPO-accountable** and aligned to PRIN 6 / PRIN 4.

**Stakeholder consensus**: DPO + NOC reconciled via the TTL compromise (REQ Conflict C-2). **Risk appetite**: privacy appetite is Very Low; this option is the only one within it.

## 7. Consequences

### 7.1 Positive

- ✅ No durable per-subscriber location anywhere (Outcome O-3); R-7 residual Low.
- ✅ Live location still available transiently for dispatch/correlation.
- ✅ Regulatory acceptability (PDPA/GDPR) — trust with operator and DPO.

**Measurable outcomes**: store-inspection test → zero durable subscriber-location records.

### 7.2 Negative (Accepted Trade-offs)

- ❌ On-demand resolution latency; TTL job complexity.
- **Mitigation**: cache within TTL only; cover the job with the privacy test.

### 7.3 Neutral (Changes Needed)

- 🔄 Schema must omit durable subscriber-location types; 🔄 a transient projection job; 🔄 a store-inspection test in CI.

### 7.4 Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
